About the equation 1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + 5 + 6 + .... = -1/12

Since about 2014 that equation has gained a lot of attention from the general public, mainly because of a YouTube video that went viral ( > 7 million views since then). That was followed by many reactions and follow-up videos, and the equation has since been discussed in mainstream magazine articles and in blogs by famously brilliant mathematicians.

This topic is interesting for several reasons. For one thing, it's obviously counter-intuitive. (If you add up bigger and bigger positive numbers all the way to infinity, the result can't be a small, negative number, right?)

Yet, in some way, it makes sense in certain branches of higher mathematics and physics. The equation has a huge celebrity quotient. It is linked with the contributions of great minds from Srinivasa Ramanujan to Bernhard Riemann to Leonhard Euler. Brilliant modern mathematicians like Terence Tao have discussed it in their blogs. The New York Times ran an article on it.

Infinity in the Physical World

One real-world killer app that's powered by this equation is a phenomenon in quantum physics called the Casimir Effect. This effect predicts that when we hold two gold plates parallel to each other less than a millionth of a meter apart, a small force is created that tries to move the plates closer together. In practical terms, the Casimir Force is a thing that designers of nano-scale mechanisms have to deal with, because it can actually interfere with the device's operation.

When you try to calculate the Casimir force between two parallel plates, you get an equation that tells you that the force depends on a certain amount of energy, which in turn is made up of an infinite number of tiny "lumps" or quanta of energy (more later).

That total energy is proportional to 1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + 5 + .... , adding up all the integers to infinity. This suggests that the force must be infinitely strong, which of course it isn't, not really. But it does turn out that you can get the correct answer if you just pretend that 1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + 5 + ... is equal to -1/12.

To many people, the fact that you can predict a physical effect using this strange equation is what makes it an irresistible challenge to understand. Certainly, that's how I felt when I watched those videos and began trying to make sense of all the material that's online.

Towards Infinity...

To start with, we will explore some key concepts around the 1 + 2 + 3 + ... equation in a physically intuitive way. We will then connect those concepts to the Casimir Force. Subsequent videos will explore other related topics, and we will be adding more experiments to the virtual lab as well.

As a rough road map, there are actually three overlapping but distinct ideas to navigate. Our first couple of videos will address the first two points below:

  • The number -1/12 has a special connection with the infinite series of integers, 1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + 5 + ...
  • There are certain physics problems that you can solve successfully if you just pretend that:
      1 + 2 + 3 + 4 + 5 + .... is equal to - 1 / 12.
  • In some specific contexts in higher mathematics, it makes sense to define the sum of an infinite series like this one in a certain special way. Once we adopt that definition, the result -1/12 will pop out of the maths.

We will explore that last point in a future video (with experiments that we will add as well).
